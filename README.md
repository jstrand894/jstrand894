# Jackson Strand

I'm a PhD student in Ecology & Environmental Science at Montana State University in Bozeman, MT, working at the intersection of field ecology, chemical ecology, and applied entomology. My dissertation focuses on biological control of invasive toadflax (*Linaria* spp.) in forest and rangeland systems of the American West, with a particular interest in how plant-insect interactions, herbivory, and volatile chemistry shape biocontrol efficacy using endophytic weevils (*Mecinus* and *Rhinusa*). I completed my M.S. in Entomology at MSU in 2024, where I studied wheat stem sawfly and native parasitoid communities in Montana agroecosystems.
